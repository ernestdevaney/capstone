Introduction

 

Cyclistic is a bike-sharing program featuring 5,800 bicycles and 600 docking stations. Daily rates are available, as well as monthly memberships. For the purpose of this project, daily users will be referred to as casual, while those with a monthly membership will be known as members.

 

Cyclistics finance analysts have concluded that annual members are much more profitable than casual riders. Although the pricing flexibility helps Cyclistic attract more customers, it is believed that maximizing the number of annual members will be key to future growth. Rather than creating a marketing campaign that targets all-new customers, it is believed that there is an opportunity to convert casual riders into members.

 

The questions posed are:

 

1.     How do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?

2.     Why would casual riders buy Cyclistic annual memberships?

3.     How can Cyclistic convert casual riders to become members?

 

Cyclistic is a fictional company, but for this project, we will be using actual data provided by Motivate International under this license: https://ride.divvybikes.com/data-license-agreement

 

Link to the datasets used: https://divvy-tripdata.s3.amazonaws.com/index.html

 

 

Process

 

I used RStudio to combine, clean, and analyze the data. I then used Tableau Public to analyze further and to create visualizations. Tableau Prep was used to wrangle data at this point.

 

 

Microsoft Excel

 

After downloading twelve months worth of data, I opened each sheet in Excel to take a quick look at what I was going to be dealing with. I also made sure that all of the columns were the same throughout all twelve files.

 

 

RStudio

Since I was dealing with millions of rows of data, I went with R instead of attempting to do much in Excel.

 

1.     I loaded all twelve files and looked at the structure of each to look for inconsistencies in variable names and string types.

 

2.     I combined all twelve sets into one.

 

3.     Checked for duplicates.

 

4.     Formalized the date format and split it into columns for day of month, month, and year.

 

5.     I created a column for ride length, in seconds.

 

6.     I removed trips shorter than two minutes, and all rows with blank station names.

 

7.     Removed all rows with docked bike as a ride type.

 

8.     After some light exploration, I exported my cleaned data set to .csv.

Recommendations

 

       A monthly email/newsletter featuring local events. Emphasize the proximity of each event to nearby bike stations and the fact that they wont have to look for/pay for parking upon arrival. The newsletter could include discounts for local restaurants, theaters, attractions, museums, etc. Examples: Show your Membership Pass to the Childrens Museum for a Buy-One-Get-One-Free discount. Show your Membership Pass at the nearby movie theater for a free large soda with any ticket purchase.

 

       The most popular station for Casuals is right outside of the Chicago Childrens Museum on the bustling Navy Pier. On the Museums website, they list various methods of transport to their location, but Cyclistic is not mentioned. Consider asking the Museum, and other local businesses at Navy Pier, to add Cyclistic as an option alongside their directions for arrival via car, public transport, etc.

 

       Recommend offering a family membership.

 

       An ad campaign stressing togetherness, being present, getting exercise, going on adventures, no parking fees, no traffic, eco-friendly, and reminding people that life is a journey.

 

       Annual Membership could include a specific amount of free or discounted day passes for guests, the way that theme park annual passes often do.

 

       If possible, offer a discount on an annual membership when a customer is returning a bike, taking at least the cost of the daily pass out of the membership.
